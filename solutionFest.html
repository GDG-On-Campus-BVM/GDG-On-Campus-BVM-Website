What is Solution Fest 2.0?

Solution Fest 2.0 invites and encourages students from all over the campus to build a solution that aligns with and aims to address the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals. We are motivating participants to learn new technologies, create solutions for real-world challenges, and explore their imagination to develop their skills. It is a "learn and solve" or "solve and learn" type of hackathon.

Goals

Testing your abilities: Hackathons enable your colleagues to enhance their soft skills such as communication, leadership, and collaboration, among others. Depending on the problem being addressed, it also provides an opportunity to sharpen technical skills like adopting new work philosophies, project management, and various methodologies.

Rounds in the Event

  • Round 1(Registration and Idea Submission)
    • Registering and Submitting the Idea and defining the Solution in clear descriptive manner. Also specify which Google Technologies does your Solution will use. Team will be shortlisted only when the definition of Solution matches the UN's 17 Sustainable Development Goals and team must be registered in Solution Challenge.

  • Round 2(Final Showdown)
    • This round will be final showdown for the shortlisted teams to showcase their skills. In this round, shortlisted teams will prepared a prototype of Solution within specified period of time. And then they will submit the prototype of Solution. On basis of work done and how much impact the Solution bring in, Judges will assess the Solution. Judges will also checks does provided Solution uses specified Google Technologies by the team. The best Solution will be declared winner of Solution Fest 2.0

Guidelines For Event

    Solution Fest 2.0 is open to all students of BVM.
    Each team must have 1 or up to 4 members, allowing diverse departmental representation within teams.
    There is no registration or entry fee required to participate in Solution Fest 2.0.
    Teams are required to select and address one of the UN's 17 Sustainable Development Goals with their proposed solution.
    Judging will occur in two rounds: Round 1 involves reviewing the proposed solution, while Round 2 focuses on evaluating the submitted Solution Prototype.
    Winners will be determined and announced by a competent judging panel after the conclusion of Round 2.
    Submissions made after the Round 1 deadline will not be accepted under any circumstances.
    During Round 2, teams are expected to present a solution that satisfactorily addresses the judges' assessment criteria.
    The Solution Fest 2.0 organizing team reserves the right to disqualify participants who fail to meet eligibility criteria or engage in any form of malpractice.
    Individuals are prohibited from participating in more than one team.
    Participants should be prepared to provide documentation or verification of the originality and development process of their solution upon request by the organizing team or judges.
    All participants must adhere to a code of conduct promoting respectful behavior, ethical conduct, and professionalism throughout the duration of the event.
